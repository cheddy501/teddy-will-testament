How To Make A Valid Will

Many people believe they have little of value and therefore don’t see the need to make a will. Additionally, facing our own mortality can be uncomfortable, leading some to avoid the topic altogether. Some may also worry that others will know the contents of their will and cause problems while they’re still alive. Finally, some have misconceptions about the legal process, believing that they must record their will somewhere or have it read aloud like in Hollywood movies.

However, in Belize and most other jurisdictions, none of these concerns are valid reasons to avoid making a will. Regardless of your assets, it’s important to create a clear plan for how they should be distributed after you pass away. You don’t need to share the details of your will with anyone if you don’t want to, and there is no requirement to record or read it aloud.

Making a will is an important step in ensuring your assets are distributed according to your wishes. Don’t let misconceptions or discomfort prevent you from taking this important action.

Financial advisors and attorneys alike counsel that making a will is important because just as we like to be in control of our assets in life, we may want to have a say in what happens to them after we die. Without a will, we have no say in the matter. Creating a will is an act of love that allows us to guide our loved ones in carrying out our wishes and protecting the interests of our heirs. Passing away without a will leaves us “intestate,” meaning the High Court must step in and make all decisions.

Belize has strict laws and requirements regarding wills. It’s not enough to simply write a will on a piece of paper or letter – it must be signed in the presence of two witnesses who are both present at the same time. The witnesses cannot be beneficiaries under the will, though they do not need to be Justices of the Peace. When creating a will, it’s important to clearly identify yourself and appoint an executor – someone you trust to manage your estate after you pass. The executor can be a beneficiary or not, but should be someone you believe will do a good job. You can also include your burial or cremation preferences in your will.

Creating a valid will is an important step in ensuring that your wishes are carried out after you pass away. Take the time to understand the legal requirements for wills in your jurisdiction, and seek the guidance of an attorney or financial advisor if needed.

For those who are not from Belize but have assets here, it’s a good idea to have a will in place. It can be made in Belize or anywhere else, as long as it follows the legal guidelines of Belize. And there’s no need to go through any extra authentication steps by Belizean authorities. For these and the reasons enumerated here, it is easier to get an attorney at law to prepare your will. Having a will helps make things easier for everyone involved.
